What is a microsite?

According to experts at SMACK creative digital agency, a microsite is a branded webpage or a group of webpages, that exist separate from your main website. The idea behind a microsite is to offer yet another platform for potential customers to discover your brand with the objective that the unique content it holds will drive them to perform an action. This action can range anywhere from simply reading the content to signing up for email newsletters. While a microsite will often contain your brand components (logo, color schemes, fonts etc.), you have the option of varying these to make the microsite standout from your normal website or to drive home a specific message.

Standard site design for 99.9%

Most City of Milwaukee departments and programs do NOT require a microsite. The official standard website provides a clean, fresh design and responsive, mobile-friendly layouts for department homepages, landing pages and subpages that is most effective for generating leads and creating conversions for our many vital initiatives.

So when is a microsite necessary?

Our partners at Northwoods advises that programs may require their own microsite:

  • To highlight specific products, sub-brands, contests, or markets
  • To target a specific audience or set of keywords
  • To enhance brands of certain products and services
